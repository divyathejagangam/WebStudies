A Salmon Belly Skewer Meal

As any experienced cook knows, fat is flavor. The problem is that while fat provides a wonderful taste explosion, too much fat can overwhelm a diner as the meal continues. The trick to maintaining the diner’s enjoyment throughout the meal is to complement the central fatty dish with acidic side dishes that break the rich flavor of fat.

In this meal, we complement the rich taste of salmon belly with a salad containing a fruity vinaigrette. For the cereal base, we introduce a garlic toast. Salmon belly is a much sought after ingredient in much of Asia, but is not commonly enjoyed in the US. Here in Rolla, you can’t find it listed for sale, but can get it from Price Chopper- just ask the fishmonger for it.

The meal described is filling for two people. Preparation of the meal begins about two to three hours before eating, but the actual early preparation work is minimal. The steps are:

Prepare the tare

Tare (タレーTeriyaki sauce) is a common base ingredient in Japanese cooking. A good shufu (主婦/主夫- homemaker) will prepare their tare from scratch rather than buying it from the store; store tare has a strange taste. However, here in Rolla, many of the core ingredients of tare, notably sake and mirin are not easy to procure. Cooking mirin can be bought here, but as any good cook knows, for alcohol, if you can’t drink it, don’t cook with it.

This recipe substitutes the basic ingredients with ingredients available in Rolla. Tare can be prepared days in advance and used for other dishes

Ingredients

Ingredient Amount
Soy Sauce 1 cup
White Wine 1 cup
Honey Half cup
Garlic 3 cloves minced
Ginger 2 inches, minced

Instructions

Combine all ingredients and bring to a boil. Allow to reduce to about half or less. It has been reduced enough when a spoonful of the mixture has the consistency of cough syrup. Strain out the garlic and ginger. When allowed to cool, it will become sticky like molasses.

Prepare the bamboo skewers

Take 8 bamboo skewers and soak in a paper towel for at least 2 hours. It is important to do this so the skewers absorb enough water they do not burn on the grill.

Make the lingonberry vinaigrette

Lingonberry jam is available from Price Chopper and can be used for other dishes like Swedish meatballs.

Ingredients

Ingredient Amount
Olive oil 1/2 cup
Apple cider vinegar 1/2 cup
Lingonberry Jam 1/4 cup
Honey To taste

Instructions

Combine olive oil, vinegar and jam. Mix well to allow vinegar to emulsify the oil. Taste and add honey/vinegar to adjust to desired level of sweetness/sourness. Note it is important that this be tart to offset the oiliness of the salmon belly.

Make the salad

This is a simple salad for two

Ingredients

Ingredient Amount
Baby arugula 2.5 oz (about half a standard sized box)
Beefsteak tomatoes 2, diced
Chopped walnuts A handful
Lingonberry vinaigrette To taste

Instructions

Lay the arugula for use as the base of the salad. Layer on the tomatoes and walnuts. Drizzle a generous amount of vinaigrette on top.

Make the garlic toast

The important thing about garlic toast is to not allow the garlic to burn. This requires that we don’t chop our garlic too finely and manage the heat of our pan. While it is delightful to watch the pan sizzle, this should not occur, or occur only minimally.

Ingredients

Ingredient Amount
White Bread 2 slices
Butter 2 tbsp
Garlic 3 cloves, slicedl

Instructions

Turn pan heat to low. Melt one tbsp butter in the pan. When the butter is half melting, add the garlic. Cook until butter is fully melted and garlic infuses the butter. Make a space in the center of the pan for the bread. Add the bread to the pan and turn heat to high. Cook for one minute then flip bread. Add second tbsp of butter to the pan. Allow to melt and the bread to absorb the butter- this way, both sides of the bread will be coated with butter. Cook for one minute, then flip bread every 30 seconds until bread is browned to desired doneness. Remove bread and layer garlic on top.

Make the salmon belly skewers

The star of the show. Only a few ingredients, but oh, so tasty!

Ingredient Amount
Salmon Belly 1 lb
Salt and pepper To taste
Skewers 8
Tare sauce 1/2 cup

Instructions

Cut salmon belly into 1 inch by 3 inch strips. Season the meat with salt and pepper. Fold the salmon belly and thread them into two skewers each. The skin of salmon is tough, and there is no need to push skewers through the skin. Instead, pierce each belly piece from the side. Make sure there is a space between each piece of meat on the skewer to allow for air to circulate, thereby allowing for more even cooking.

Fire up the grill and allow to reach an internal temperature of at least 450F. Place skewers on hottest part of the grill, cover, and allow to sit for at least 2 minutes- more if you want your salmon belly more thoroughly cooked. Open grill, flip skewers and cover for 2 minutes. Open the grill, move the skewers to the cooler parts of the grill. Paint one side of the skewers with tare, flip and allow to cook until tare becomes shiny (about one minute). Paint the other side of the skewer and repeat.

Can be served immediately
